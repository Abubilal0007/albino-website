Frequently Asked Questions

Albinism is a genetic condition that results in a lack of melanin, the pigment that colors the skin, hair, and eyes. Animals with albinism have white or light-colored fur and pink eyes.

Survival can be difficult for albino animals in the wild. Their lack of camouflage makes them more vulnerable to predators, and they may also have vision problems due to the lack of pigment in their eyes.

Albino animals completely lack melanin, resulting in pink eyes and pale skin or fur. Leucistic animals have reduced pigmentation, but they may still have some color and normal eye pigmentation.
